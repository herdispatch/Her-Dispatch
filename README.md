# Her Dispatch 🌍

> *Reporting the lives of African women and girls the world hasn’t heard yet.*

-----

## About

**Her Dispatch** is an African-led storytelling and advocacy organization that finds, amplifies, and deepens the stories of women and girls living at the margins of visibility across East Africa — and then goes further.

We are not a charity. We are not a rescue organization. We are a dispatch — filed from the field, urgent, truthful, and impossible to ignore.

The stories of African women and girls are not missing from the world. They are everywhere — in community radio stations across Turkana, in the WhatsApp groups of Mathare, in the notebooks of local journalists working with limited resources and even more limited reach.

**The problem is not that these stories don’t exist. The problem is they don’t travel far enough.**

Her Dispatch was built to close that gap.

## How to Use

### View Locally

Simply download `index.html` and open it in any web browser. No dependencies, no build tools, no installation required.

### Deploy

This is a static single-file site. It can be deployed anywhere:

- **Netlify Drop** — drag and drop `index.html` at netlify.com/drop. Live in 60 seconds.
- **GitHub Pages** — already here. Go to Settings → Pages → set source to main branch.
- **Carrd** — paste the code into an Embed block.
- **Any static host** — upload the file and you are done.

## Our Model

### Phase 1 — Find & Tell *(Year 1)*

Travel to ASAL communities and Nairobi informal settlements. Spend real time with women and girls — days, not hours. Produce deeply reported multimedia stories. Place them in major local and international publications.

### Phase 2 — Train & Amplify *(Year 2+)*

The women and girls we feature become our first storytelling fellows. Teach them basic journalism using their phones. They become the narrators of their own communities.

### Phase 3 — Connect & Change *(Ongoing)*

Their collective voices become evidence. Presented to county assemblies, parliaments, and international donors. Their stories connect them to mentors, scholarships, and opportunities.
